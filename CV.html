Selected Experience

Quantum Computing Research Intern
Hon Hai Quantum Computing Research Centre
Jan 2023 - Present

Working under Dr. Min-Hsiu Hsieh, I am investigating quantum error-correcting codes with a particular focus on local testability. I started by covering a broad range of literature relevant to qLDPC codes and qLTCs from January onwards. We have recently been able to apply the general distance balancing construction of Evra et al. to qLTCs to obtain a new construction for qLTCs, as well as codes of new parameters. These results can be found on arXiv.

Summer Research in the CMS Project (Quantum Computing)
Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics, University of Cambridge
Jul 2022 - Dec 2022

Supervised by Dr. Sergii Strelchuk and funded by the Royal Society, I investigated various questions relating to permutational quantum computing and the Schur transform. With this work, we discovered the first elementary and efficient algorithm for the quantum Schur transform and developed a new formalism to examine the efficient implementation and classical simulability of generally coupled quantum states. With these findings, Dr. Strelchuk and I co-authored a paper.

Master's Essay: Symmetry Protected Quantum Computing
Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics, University of Cambridge
Dec 2021 - May 2022

As partial credit for Part III of the Cambridge Mathematical Tripos, I wrote an essay on permutational quantum computing, symmetry protected quantum computing and their interplay, supervised by Dr. Sergii Strelchuk, that scored 92%.

Summer Research in the CMS Project (Relativity)
Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics, University of Cambridge
Jul 2021 - Sep 2021

Working with the Numerical Relativity Group for 8 weeks and funded by the Summer Research in the CMS Programme, I focused my project on performing perturbation theory on a two-way wormhole spacetime to extract the quasinormal frequencies of the object. I presented my findings to the research group and at the CMS Festival of Summer Research and was praised by the group leader - Prof. Ulrich Sperhake.
